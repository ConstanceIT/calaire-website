## Design Context

### Users
The "Managing Everything" Woman — ages 32–50, $150K+ household income, affluent Austin neighborhoods. She's experiencing burnout, decision fatigue, and isolation. She browses on her phone during rare quiet moments (kids napping, late at night) or on a laptop during a work break. She's not casually browsing — she's looking for permission to take a full day for herself. She needs to feel that this is worth it, that it's handled, and that she won't feel guilty.

### Brand Personality
**Three words:** Warm. Intentional. Grounded.

**Voice:** A trusted friend who happens to know a lot about wellness — not a guru, not an influencer, not a spa brochure. Speaks with understated confidence and restraint. Luxury that doesn't beg.

**Emotional goals:** Relief ("someone gets it"), trust ("everything is handled"), desire ("I want to be there"), and belonging ("this is for women like me") — without forced community or spiritual language.

### Aesthetic Direction
**Visual tone:** Refined luxury with warmth. Think Aesop, Aman, The Row — brands that speak with restraint and let quality speak for itself. Not cold minimalism; not boho warmth. The intersection: considered, calm, premium, human.

**References:**
- Eagle's Nest (eaglesnest.sergesyutkin.com) — oversized typography, ivory + sage, retreat positioning. Highest relevance.
- Aquamare Marine (aquamaremarine.com) — large headings, cream palette, luxury hospitality feel.
- Core Atelier Pilates (coreatelierpilates.com) — warm blush palette, wellness-specific. Closest vertical match.

**Anti-references:**
- Neon gradients or tech-startup aesthetics
- "Sacred sisterhood" crystal-and-candle imagery
- Generic Squarespace wellness templates
- Forced spiritual language or woo-woo visual styling
- Equal-width card grids, SaaS layouts, generic template structures

**Theme:** Light. Warm ivory/cream (#FAF6F1) backgrounds. This product is used by women browsing during daylight hours or in well-lit rooms. Light mode communicates openness, calm, and warmth.

**Typography:**
- Headings: Cormorant Garamond (Medium 500) — serif, elegant, French character. Uppercase for display. Minimum 120px H1 on desktop.
- Body: Raleway (Light 300) — geometric sans-serif, clean complement. Mixed case.
- These are non-negotiable brand fonts. Override any skill recommendation to change them.

**Color:**
- Primary: Deep Burgundy/Wine #5E3038 — brand color, headings, accents, CTAs
- Background: Warm Ivory/Cream #FAF6F1 — primary background
- Reversed: White #FFFFFF — text on dark backgrounds only
- Print/Mono: Black #000000 — single-color applications
- Derive all supporting colors (hover states, borders, muted text) from these base values.

### Design Principles
1. **Calm over clever.** Every element should lower the visitor's heart rate, not raise it. No visual noise, no competing elements, no anxiety-inducing urgency.
2. **Space is the product.** Generous whitespace isn't wasted — it communicates the same luxury and breathing room that Calaire's retreat provides. Let content breathe.
3. **Show, don't sell.** The copy and layout should make the visitor feel what the day is like, not pitch her on features. Sensory language, editorial pacing, not marketing bullet points.
4. **Decisions already made.** The site mirrors the retreat's value prop: everything is handled. Clear hierarchy, obvious next steps, zero cognitive overhead.
5. **Earned attention.** Content reveals on scroll, not all at once. Each section earns the visitor's attention sequentially, building desire through pacing, not information overload.
